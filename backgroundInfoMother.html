What is a gene?

  • A sequence of DNA that is given from parent to offspring
  • Has instructions for characteristics or traits
    • Ex: hair color, eye color, and skin color

What is an allele?

  • A variation/subcategory of the gene
    • Ex: blue eyes and brown eyes

Genes vs. Characteristics

Genotype: the letter combinations of genes

Phenotype: the characteristic (physical appearance)

Which trait shows?

  • Dominant: the trait that will always appear in the offspring if present in the genotype
    • Represented by a capital letter
    • At least one capital letter
    • Ex: BB or Bb
    • Not always the most common trait in the population
      • Ex: Huntingtonâ€™s disease is a dominant trait but only affects about 30,000 people in the U.S.

  • Recessive: the trait that can hide in offspring unless there are two copies of it
    • Represented by a lowercase letter
    • The trait can skip generations
    • Ex: bb
